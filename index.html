Automatic Film Processor
Show Me More
-->

You clip your pre-exposed photographic paper into the paper holder and insert the holding in to holder arm of th device. You can add up to 5 liquids for processor. Usually 4 chemicals and water is used to develop black and white images and 3 chemcials and water is used to develop coloured images. The time that the paper spends in each chemical can be altered depending on the look that you want. The integrated pump which blows bubbles into each container makes your that each fluid is irrigated and the heating element controlled by the thermostat keeps the liquid at a constant temperature.

Fumes

Tradionally the fumes emitted by the chemicals cause lightheadness. You don't have to worry about fumes as the BlackBox can be left to work alone when switched on.

Darkness

Fumbling around in the dark can be hard when developing photos, especially when developing coloured photos as you are in pitch black. You can leave the BlackBox in a dark room annd let it work its magic

Timing

If you are going for a desired look and want to change the exposure timings, not to worry as the dials allow you to change the amount of time that is spent in each container.

About

Oh so you want to know how it all started? Once upon a time...

  • November 2019

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Our friend, Aiden, who is a darkroom technician in Imperial College London Photography society, was tired of working in low-light to dark conditions trying to get the perfect picture. The fumes emitted from the chemicals would often make him light headed, and he had to submerge the photographic paper for just the right amount of time to get the perfect picture. There was also the risk of back contaminating the liquids.

  • Bad Photos

    If he didn't do the process just right he would often get bad results such as this. There would be areas of the picture that were underdevloped or overdeveloped. He was tired of it.

  • December 2019

    An Idea is Born!

    That is when our Chief Technical Officer, Campbell, came up with the concept of Blackbox. A way to automate the development stage of photographs after it has been exposed.

  • Development

    We went to the design room and started working and prototyping multiple solutions. After many....

GDPR
Definition: GDPR stands for 'General Data Protection Regulation' and is a legal data protection and privacy framework upheld by the European Union which defines how personal information of EU residents is processed, and can be seen as an expansion of the Data Protection Act 1998. EU residents have autonomy over their data and can request a copy of any of their stored personal data. They can also request to be “forgotten” by entities that hold their data.
EU Residents have the following rights under GDPR, therefore the following must be upheld:
1. Right to be informed
Be transparent about the intended use of personal data. Typically handled through the site’s privacy policy.
2. Right of access
Data must be provided on request, and should be provided in commonly used format such as CSV.
3. Right to rectification
Allow a client to correct incomplete or inaccurate information.
4. Right to erasure
Clients can request deletion or removal of personal data when there is no compelling reason for its continued processing. Also referred to as “the right to be forgotten.”
5. Right to restrict processing
Data can be stored but no longer processed if clients block the processing of their personal data.
6. Right to portability
Allow individuals to obtain and reuse their personal data for their own purposes.
7. Right to object
Individuals can object to having their personal information used. This includes for purposes of direct marketing, research and statistics.
8. Rights related to automatic decision making, including profiling
This rule specifies when you can use profiling and automated decision making. It also defines requirements that must be met, such as the individual providing explicit consent.
